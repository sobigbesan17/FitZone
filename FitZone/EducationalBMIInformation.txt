Underweight Description:
---
A BMI below 18.5 is classified as Underweight. This indicates that your body weight is lower than what is considered healthy for your height.

Health Risks Associated with Being Underweight:
• Nutritional deficiencies (vitamins, minerals, and essential nutrients)
• Weakened immune system, making you more susceptible to infections
• Osteoporosis and bone density loss
• Anaemia (low red blood cell count)
• Fertility issues and hormonal imbalances
• In severe cases, organ failure

Recommendations:
If you are classified as underweight, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian. They can help you develop a balanced nutrition plan to reach and maintain a healthy weight. Focus on nutrient-dense foods such as lean proteins, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. Avoid crash diets or rapid weight gain approaches.

Normal Weight Description:
---
A BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is classified as Normal Weight. Congratulations! Your weight is within the healthy range for your height.

Benefits of Maintaining a Healthy BMI:
• Reduced risk of cardiovascular disease and hypertension
• Lower risk of type 2 diabetes
• Better joint health and reduced risk of arthritis
• Improved energy levels and overall wellbeing
• Reduced risk of certain cancers
• Better mental health outcomes

Recommendations:
Maintain your healthy weight through a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Regular physical activity (at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week) is essential. Continue monitoring your BMI periodically and make adjustments to your lifestyle as needed.

Overweight Description:
---
A BMI between 25 and 29.9 is classified as Overweight. This suggests that your body weight is above the healthy range for your height, which may increase health risks.

Health Risks Associated with Being Overweight:
• Increased risk of cardiovascular disease
• Higher risk of developing type 2 diabetes
• Elevated blood pressure (hypertension)
• Increased cholesterol levels
• Joint pain and increased risk of osteoarthritis
• Sleep apnoea and breathing difficulties
• Increased risk of certain cancers

Recommendations:
Even modest weight loss of 5-10% of your body weight can significantly reduce health risks. Focus on creating a calorie deficit through a combination of improved diet and regular exercise. Consider consulting a registered dietitian for personalised meal planning. Incorporate at least 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week. Strength training 2-3 times per week can also be beneficial.

Obese (Class 1) Description:
---
A BMI between 30 and 34.9 is classified as Obese Class 1. This level of obesity is associated with significant health risks and requires attention.

Health Risks Associated with Class 1 Obesity:
• High risk of cardiovascular disease and heart attacks
• Significantly elevated risk of type 2 diabetes
• Severe hypertension (high blood pressure)
• High cholesterol and triglyceride levels
• Increased risk of stroke
• Sleep apnoea
• Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
• Increased risk of multiple types of cancer
• Joint problems, particularly in knees and hips

Recommendations:
Class 1 obesity is a serious medical condition that warrants consultation with healthcare professionals. A comprehensive weight management programme including dietary changes, increased physical activity, and behavioural therapy is recommended. In some cases, medication may be considered under medical supervision. Even small amounts of weight loss can lead to significant health improvements.
---
Obese (Class 2) Description:
---
A BMI between 35 and 39.9 is classified as Obese Class 2. This is a higher level of obesity that carries very high health risks and requires medical attention.

Health Risks Associated with Class 2 Obesity:
• Very high risk of life-threatening cardiovascular events
• Severe type 2 diabetes with potential complications
• Severe hypertension potentially leading to organ damage
• Respiratory problems and severe sleep apnoea
• Increased risk of multiple cancers
• Severe joint and mobility issues
• Depression and other mental health conditions
• Reduced life expectancy

Recommendations:
Class 2 obesity requires immediate medical intervention. Please consult with your GP or a specialist in obesity medicine. Treatment options may include medically supervised weight loss programmes, prescription weight loss medication, and in some cases, bariatric surgery may be considered. Psychological support is also an important component of treatment.
---
Obese (Class 3) Description:
---
A BMI of 40 or above is classified as Obese Class 3, sometimes referred to as severe or morbid obesity. This level of obesity is associated with extremely high health risks and significantly reduced life expectancy.

Health Risks Associated with Class 3 Obesity:
• Extremely high risk of fatal cardiovascular events
• Severe and often uncontrolled type 2 diabetes
• Hypertension with organ damage
• Severe respiratory failure
• High risk of multiple types of cancer
• Complete loss of mobility in some cases
• Severe psychological impact including depression and anxiety
• Significantly reduced life expectancy (10-20 years less)

Recommendations:
Class 3 obesity is a medical emergency that requires urgent intervention from a multidisciplinary healthcare team. Bariatric surgery (such as gastric bypass or sleeve gastrectomy) may be the most effective treatment option and can lead to significant, sustained weight loss. This should be combined with intensive dietary counselling, psychological support, and regular medical monitoring. Please seek immediate medical help from your GP or healthcare provider.
